What is CodaFest?

A Modern Gathering, Rooted in the Old Deaf Clubs

Held at Camp Taloali in Stayton, Oregon, CodaFest brings people together for a weekend that blends education, storytelling, cultural exchange, and time in nature. This is not a traditional conference — it's a camp-style experience designed to feel human, accessible, and grounded in real life.

Many families live between Deaf and hearing worlds. Codas grow up navigating both — often without spaces that fully reflect their experience. CodaFest creates a space where these experiences can come together side by side: a place to connect, laugh, share stories, and reconnect with people who understand.

Join us — Codas, Deaf parents, hearing family & friends — to celebrate our Deaf community, culture, and connection.

Find Us

Camp Taloali

15934 N Santiam Hwy SE
Stayton, OR 97383

Camp Taloali is a Deaf-owned property nestled along the North Santiam River — also home to the World Deaf Timberfest. It's the perfect grounded, accessible setting for a camp-style weekend.

Getting Here

About 20 minutes east of Salem. Detailed driving and arrival instructions are sent with your registration confirmation.

Accessibility

🤟 CodaFest is built for full language access. ASL interpreters and a Deaf-centered environment throughout the weekend. Questions about specific access needs? Email codafestthat@gmail.com.
