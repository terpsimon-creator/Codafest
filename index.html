What is CodaFest?

Not a conference.
A gathering.

CodaFest is a Deaf-family owned and operated camp-style event held at Camp Taloali — a Deaf-owned property on the North Santiam River. For 4 days, Codas, Deaf parents, hearing family, and allies come together for something deeply human: stories, laughter, learning, and belonging.

All-language access with ASL interpreters throughout all programming. CEUs & clock hours available for qualifying professionals. Youth Camp (ages 7–17) runs its own track — K-Pop Demon Hunter themed. Children 0–6 attend free.

RID CEU Processing

CodaFest workshops are designed to meet RID (Registry of Interpreters for the Deaf) continuing education requirements. Professional members receive official CEU documentation, completion certificates, and attendance records for direct submission to RID. Our RID Professional tier includes full documentation support to streamline the CEU reporting process for working interpreters and Deaf services professionals.

Our Mission

What We Stand For

CodaFest is a community-centered gathering dedicated to connection, cultural understanding, and the recognition of Codas as a unique and vital part of the Deaf ecosystem.

We stand in active support of the fight against language injustice, advocating for full language access, equity, and recognition across Deaf and hearing systems.

By bringing families and communities together, we create space for healing, leadership, and collective movement toward a more just and inclusive future.

🧠 Wellness

Supporting emotional, relational, and personal wellbeing through reflection, connection, and shared understanding.

🎭 Coda Culture

Celebrating the vibrancy, humor, resilience, and creativity that are part of Coda life and identity.

⚖️ Language Justice

Advocating for full language access, cultural respect, and equity for Deaf people and their families.

🤝 Collaboration

Bringing Codas, Deaf community, professionals, and allies together to build stronger, inclusive communities.
